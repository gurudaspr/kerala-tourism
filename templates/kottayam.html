KOTTAYAM

TOP DESTINATIONS

Vagamon

Vagamon is a serene and idyllic hill station that captivates visitors with its lush green landscapes and tranquil ambiance. Surrounded by rolling hills, meadows, and pine forests, Vagamon is a perfect retreat for those seeking a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The destination is characterized by its pleasant climate, making it a year-round destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Vagamon's attractions include the Vagamon Meadows, dotted with vibrant flowers and offering panoramic views of the surrounding hills, and the pine-covered valleys that add to the area's scenic beauty. The region also offers opportunities for trekking, paragliding, and boating, providing adventure enthusiasts with an array of outdoor activities. With its unspoiled natural beauty and a serene atmosphere, Vagamon has gained popularity as a hidden gem in Kerala, providing a refreshing and rejuvenating experience for those who venture into its peaceful embrace.

Vembanad Lake

Vembanad Lake is the largest lake in the state and one of the longest in the country. Spanning several districts, including Alappuzha, Kottayam, and Ernakulam, this picturesque water body is an integral part of Kerala's intricate backwater network. The lake is renowned for its vast expanse, serene backwaters, and rich biodiversity, making it a vital ecological and cultural landmark in the region.

Vembanad Lake is not only a stunning natural attraction but also a hub for traditional houseboat cruises, offering a unique and immersive experience of Kerala's backwater lifestyle. The surrounding landscape is adorned with coconut groves, paddy fields, and quaint villages, providing a scenic backdrop for boat journeys. The annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, held on the lake, is a major event that attracts visitors from around the world, showcasing the traditional snake boat races and adding a festive charm to the serene waters of Vembanad. With its cultural significance and breathtaking beauty, Vembanad Lake holds a special place in Kerala's tourism map, offering a tranquil escape into the heart of the state's backwater charm.

Kumarakom

Kumarakom is a serene and idyllic backwater destination celebrated for its natural beauty and tranquility. This picturesque village is renowned for its intricate network of canals, lagoons, and lush greenery, making it a popular retreat for those seeking a peaceful escape. The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, situated on the eastern banks of Vembanad, adds to the charm with its diverse avian population, including migratory birds, making it a paradise for birdwatchers.

Kumarakom is also famous for its luxurious houseboat cruises, allowing visitors to leisurely glide through the scenic backwaters while experiencing the traditional lifestyle of the region. The destination offers a unique blend of relaxation and cultural immersion, with traditional Kerala architecture, vibrant festivals, and delicious local cuisine adding to the overall experience. Whether it's enjoying a houseboat cruise, exploring the bird sanctuary, or simply unwinding amidst the natural beauty, Kumarakom stands as a testament to the backwater allure that defines Kerala's tourism landscape.

Illickal Kallu

Illickal Kallu is a breathtaking destination known for its stunning rock formations and panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes. The name "Illickal Kallu" translates to "Moonrock Cliff" in Malayalam, aptly describing the unique geological structure of the site. This natural wonder is characterized by three massive rocks that stand vertically atop each other, resembling a giant monolith, against the backdrop of the lush Western Ghats.

Visitors to Illickal Kallu are rewarded with awe-inspiring vistas of the Meenachil River Valley and the endless stretches of greenery that define the region. The site is not only a visual delight but also holds cultural and religious significance, featuring a small temple dedicated to Lord Rama and Lakshmana at its base. The trek to Illickal Kallu offers a blend of adventure and spirituality, as pilgrims and nature enthusiasts alike make the journey to witness the breathtaking sunrise and sunset views from this elevated vantage point. The serene ambiance, coupled with the unique geological formations, makes Illickal Kallu a hidden gem in the Western Ghats, offering a tranquil escape for those seeking natural beauty and spiritual solace.
