KOZHIKODE

TOP DESTINATIONS

Kozhikode Beach

Kozhikode Beach, also known as Calicut Beach, is a renowned coastal destination situated along the Arabian Sea in the city of Kozhikode (Calicut) in Kerala, India. This scenic beach holds historical significance as it was the landing site of the Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama in 1498, marking the beginning of European maritime trade with India. Today, Kozhikode Beach is a popular spot for both locals and tourists, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage.

The beachfront is adorned with a picturesque pier, lighthouse, and well-maintained promenade, providing a pleasant setting for leisurely walks and relaxation. Visitors can engage in various activities like beach volleyball, camel rides, and horseback riding along the shore. The local seafood stalls and eateries add a delightful culinary experience, allowing visitors to savor the flavors of Malabar cuisine. Sunset views over the Arabian Sea from Kozhikode Beach are particularly enchanting, making it a favored spot for those seeking tranquility and the allure of coastal landscapes in Kerala.

Kakkayam Dam

Kakkayam Dam, located in the Kozhikode district of Kerala, India, is a scenic reservoir surrounded by lush greenery and nestled amidst the Western Ghats. The dam, constructed across the Kakkayam River, serves as a vital water source for irrigation and hydroelectric power generation. The tranquil ambiance and natural beauty of the Kakkayam Dam make it a popular destination for both nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

Surrounded by dense forests, Kakkayam offers opportunities for trekking and hiking, allowing visitors to explore the pristine wilderness of the Western Ghats. The dam site is a gateway to the Kakkayam Valley, where one can find diverse flora and fauna. The scenic landscape, coupled with the serene reservoir, provides a serene environment for picnics and relaxation. Adventure activities such as rock climbing and rappelling are also organized in the vicinity, attracting thrill-seekers to experience the natural wonders of Kakkayam Dam. Overall, the dam and its surroundings offer a peaceful retreat for those looking to escape into the lap of nature in the northern part of Kerala.

Beypore Beach

Beypore Beach, located near Kozhikode (Calicut) in Kerala, India, is a charming coastal destination renowned for its historical significance and natural beauty. The beach holds a unique place in maritime history as it was once a prominent shipbuilding center, known for the construction of the traditional wooden sailing vessels called "urukus." The craftsmanship of the local artisans and the historical remnants of the shipbuilding industry contribute to the cultural richness of Beypore.

Visitors to Beypore Beach can explore the vibrant hustle and bustle of the local fishing community, witnessing traditional fishing techniques and the picturesque sight of fishing boats returning with their catches. The Beypore Beach is also famous for its ancient Beypore Fort, initially built by the Portuguese and later expanded by Tipu Sultan. The remnants of the fort, including the intricately designed entrance and the observation platforms, offer a glimpse into the area's historical past. With its scenic charm, historical significance, and cultural vibrancy, Beypore Beach provides a delightful experience for those seeking a blend of coastal beauty and maritime heritage in Kerala.

Mananchira

Mananchira is a historic and picturesque public square surrounded by lush greenery, providing a serene oasis in the midst of the bustling city. The centerpiece of Mananchira is the large rectangular artificial pond, built by the Zamorin rulers, which reflects the region's rich cultural heritage. The pond is flanked by well-maintained walkways, beautifully landscaped gardens, and a musical fountain, creating a peaceful retreat for both locals and tourists.

The park surrounding Mananchira hosts various cultural events, making it a hub for artistic and recreational activities. The area also features important landmarks, including the Town Hall and the Commonwealth Trust Hall. The S.M. Street, a bustling shopping district, is in close proximity, offering a vibrant atmosphere for shopping and culinary delights. Mananchira serves not only as a popular recreational spot but also as a symbol of the historical legacy of Kozhikode, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking a blend of cultural immersion and natural beauty in the heart of the city.
