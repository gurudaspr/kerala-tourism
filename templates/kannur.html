TOP DESTINATIONS

St.Angelo Fort

St. Angelo Fort, located in Kannur, Kerala, India, stands as a symbol of the region's rich historical past and maritime significance. Constructed by the Portuguese in 1505, the fort was initially named Fortaleza de São Ângelo, serving as a key strategic outpost for the Portuguese colonial empire. Over the centuries, the fort underwent several changes of ownership, with the Dutch capturing it in the 17th century before it eventually fell into the hands of the British in the 18th century.

The fort's architecture reflects a blend of European, Indian, and Islamic influences, showcasing its historical evolution and the cultural interactions in the region. Its location along the Arabian Sea offers stunning views of the coastline, making it a popular tourist destination. Today, St. Angelo Fort stands as a well-preserved historical monument, providing visitors with a glimpse into the bygone era of maritime trade, colonial rivalry, and cultural amalgamation in the coastal regions of Kerala. As the fort celebrates its rich history, it continues to attract tourists and history enthusiasts alike, contributing to Kannur's cultural heritage.

Muzhappilangad Beach

Muzhappilangad Beach, situated along the Malabar Coast in the state of Kerala, India, is renowned for being the longest drive-in beach in Asia. Stretching for about 4.5 kilometers, the beach offers a unique experience, allowing visitors to drive their vehicles directly on the firm sands of the shore. The gently sloping beach and firm terrain make it ideal for this distinctive activity, attracting both locals and tourists seeking a thrilling and scenic drive along the Arabian Sea. The beach is also known for its serene ambiance, with coconut groves lining the shoreline and the rhythmic sound of waves providing a calming backdrop.

Apart from its driving allure, Muzhappilangad Beach is a popular destination for various water sports and recreational activities. Adventure enthusiasts can indulge in activities like parasailing, jet-skiing, and beach volleyball. The beach's natural beauty, combined with its unique features and recreational opportunities, has established Muzhappilangad as a must-visit destination for those looking to experience the best of Kerala's coastal attractions.

Arakkal Museum

The Arakkal Museum is a historical treasure trove that preserves the heritage of the Arakkal family, the only Muslim royal family in Kerala. The museum is housed in the Arakkal Kettu, a traditional Malabar-style building that was the residence of the Arakkal Ali Rajas. The Arakkal dynasty, with its roots dating back to the 16th century, played a significant role in the political and cultural landscape of the Malabar region.

The museum offers visitors a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle and cultural contributions of the Arakkal rulers. It displays a fascinating collection of artifacts, including royal furniture, weapons, artifacts related to the maritime history of the region, and personal belongings of the royal family. The exhibits provide insight into the social and political history of the Malabar Coast and the unique position held by the Arakkal rulers. As a cultural repository, the Arakkal Museum stands as a testament to the rich diversity of Kerala's history and serves as a valuable educational resource for those interested in the region's royal heritage.

Paithalmala

Paithalmala is a picturesque hill station that captivates visitors with its lush greenery, rolling hills, and breathtaking landscapes. Nestled in the Western Ghats at an elevation of about 4,500 feet above sea level, Paithalmala is renowned for its enchanting trekking trails and panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and plains. The hill station is a haven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, offering a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

The trek to Paithalmala takes visitors through dense forests, tea plantations, and meandering paths that lead to the summit. Once at the top, one can witness stunning vistas of the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea. The cool climate, coupled with the tranquility of the surroundings, makes Paithalmala an ideal destination for those seeking a refreshing and rejuvenating experience. The hill station's biodiversity, encompassing a variety of flora and fauna, adds to its ecological significance. Whether it's trekking, bird-watching, or simply enjoying the serenity of nature, Paithalmala offers a memorable retreat for those exploring the natural wonders of Kerala.
