TOP DESTINATIONS

Banasura Sagar Dam

Banasura Sagar Dam is the largest earthen dam in the country and a prominent tourist attraction. Nestled amidst the scenic Western Ghats, the dam is built across the Karamanathodu River, creating an expansive reservoir with lush green surroundings. The dam derives its name from Banasura, a mythical character from Indian mythology, and the reservoir offers breathtaking views of the mist-covered hills, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts.

The dam site also serves as a gateway to the Banasura Hills, where trekking trails take visitors through dense forests, providing an opportunity to explore the rich biodiversity of the region. One of the key features of Banasura Sagar Dam is the series of islands that emerge during the monsoon season when the water level rises. Boating is a popular activity on the reservoir, allowing visitors to experience the serene beauty of the surroundings. The dam and its picturesque landscape offer a tranquil retreat, making it an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful escape amidst nature in the enchanting Wayanad region of Kerala.

Muthanga Wildlife Sanctuary

Muthanga Wildlife Sanctuary is a captivating haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. Spread across the Western Ghats, this sanctuary is an integral part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve and serves as a vital corridor for the movement of elephants between the Mudumalai National Park in Tamil Nadu and the Bandipur National Park in Karnataka. The diverse landscape of Muthanga encompasses tropical forests, grasslands, and streams, providing a rich habitat for a variety of flora and fauna.

Visitors to Muthanga Wildlife Sanctuary can embark on safari adventures, either by jeep or bus, to explore the wilderness and catch glimpses of the resident wildlife. The sanctuary is home to a diverse range of species, including elephants, deer, tigers, leopards, and numerous bird species. The conservation efforts in Muthanga aim to preserve the natural ecosystem and protect endangered species. The sanctuary not only offers a thrilling wildlife experience but also provides an opportunity for environmental education and awareness, making it a must-visit destination for those interested in the rich biodiversity of the Western Ghats.

Edakkal Caves

Beypore Beach, located near Kozhikode (Calicut) in Kerala, India, is a charming coastal destination renowned for its historical significance and natural beauty. The beach holds a unique place in maritime history as it was once a prominent shipbuilding center, known for the construction of the traditional wooden sailing vessels called "urukus." The craftsmanship of the local artisans and the historical remnants of the shipbuilding industry contribute to the cultural richness of Beypore.

Visitors to Beypore Beach can explore the vibrant hustle and bustle of the local fishing community, witnessing traditional fishing techniques and the picturesque sight of fishing boats returning with their catches. The Beypore Beach is also famous for its ancient Beypore Fort, initially built by the Portuguese and later expanded by Tipu Sultan. The remnants of the fort, including the intricately designed entrance and the observation platforms, offer a glimpse into the area's historical past. With its scenic charm, historical significance, and cultural vibrancy, Beypore Beach provides a delightful experience for those seeking a blend of coastal beauty and maritime heritage in Kerala.

Pookode Lake

Pookode Lake, nestled amid the lush greenery of Wayanad in Kerala, India, stands as a serene and enchanting freshwater lake. Surrounded by dense forests and hills, the lake is situated at an elevation of about 2,100 meters above sea level. One of the main attractions of Pookode Lake is its natural beauty and tranquility, making it a favored destination for nature lovers and those seeking a peaceful retreat.

Visitors to Pookode Lake can enjoy activities such as boating, with pedal boats and rowboats available for hire, allowing them to traverse the crystal-clear waters while soaking in the picturesque landscape. The lake is also surrounded by a well-maintained pathway, providing a pleasant setting for leisurely walks. The lush greenery and the cool climate add to the charm of Pookode Lake, making it a popular spot for picnics and family outings. With its serene ambiance and breathtaking surroundings, Pookode Lake offers a refreshing escape into nature in the heart of Wayanad.
