TOP DESTINATIONS

Thattekad Bird Sanctuary

Thattekad Bird Sanctuary, situated in the Ernakulam district of Kerala, India, is a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts. Spanning over 25 square kilometers, this sanctuary is renowned for its rich avian biodiversity, making it a paradise for bird lovers. Dr. Salim Ali, the renowned Indian ornithologist, once referred to Thattekad as the "richest bird habitat on peninsular India".

The sanctuary is predominantly covered with evergreen and deciduous forests and is crisscrossed by several streams and rivers, creating a diverse ecosystem. It is home to over 300 species of birds, including endemics like the Malabar Grey Hornbill and the Sri Lanka Frogmouth. The dense foliage and the Periyar River flowing through the sanctuary add to its scenic beauty. Visitors can explore the numerous trails within the sanctuary to catch glimpses of various bird species, making Thattekad a popular destination for ecotourism and birding in the Western Ghats.

Cherai Beach

Cherai Beach is a gem along the Arabian Sea renowned for its idyllic beauty. The beach's pristine shoreline, adorned with soft white sand and bordered by the gentle waves of the Arabian Sea, creates a serene and inviting atmosphere. Tourists flock to Cherai to bask in the sun, take refreshing swims in the clear waters, and marvel at the scenic charm that encompasses the coastline. The beach's tranquil ambiance, complemented by the backdrop of coconut groves and lush greenery, makes it a perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful escape along the captivating shores of Kerala.

Cherai Beach is not only a haven for relaxation but also offers an array of activities, including water sports like speedboating and water scooters. The unique feature of the beach, where the backwaters merge with the sea, adds to its allure. With its natural beauty, recreational opportunities, and the harmonious blend of backwaters and sea, Cherai Beach stands as a must-visit destination for those exploring the coastal wonders of Kerala.

Marine Drive

Marine Drive in Kochi, Kerala, is a captivating waterfront promenade that captivates visitors with its scenic beauty and vibrant ambiance. Stretching along the shores of the Arabian Sea, this iconic destination offers a panoramic view of the backwaters, creating a perfect setting for both relaxation and social activities. The well-designed walkways, adorned with greenery and seating areas, invite locals and tourists to enjoy leisurely strolls, take in the fresh sea breeze, and witness the enchanting sunset that paints the sky over the Arabian Sea in hues of orange and pink. The bustling activity along Marine Drive, with its mix of shops, eateries, and recreational spaces, makes it a lively hub where people gather to socialize, shop for local crafts, or simply enjoy the vibrant atmosphere.

Moreover, the presence of traditional Chinese fishing nets along the promenade adds a touch of cultural charm, providing a glimpse into the region's rich maritime heritage. Boating options further enhance the experience, allowing visitors to explore the backwaters and enjoy Kochi's scenic beauty from a different perspective. Whether for a quiet evening by the sea, a casual stroll, or engaging in the vibrant local scene, Marine Drive stands as a quintessential destination that showcases the coastal allure and cultural richness of Kochi.

Fort Kochi

Fort Kochi, situated in the historic city of Kochi, Kerala, is a charming and culturally rich neighborhood that reflects the region's colonial past. The area is renowned for its well-preserved colonial architecture, narrow streets, and a unique blend of Indian, Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences. One of the highlights of Fort Kochi is the St. Francis Church, believed to be the oldest European-built church in India, where explorer Vasco da Gama was initially buried before his remains were later transferred to Lisbon. The iconic Chinese fishing nets, dotting the coastline, are another signature feature of Fort Kochi, offering a picturesque and timeless glimpse into traditional fishing practices.

The vibrant streets of Fort Kochi are lined with art galleries, antique shops, and cafes, creating a bohemian atmosphere that attracts artists, historians, and tourists alike. The neighborhood hosts the Kochi-Muziris Biennale, a contemporary art festival, adding a modern touch to its historical charm. As visitors wander through the streets, they encounter colonial-era buildings, vibrant street art, and a unique fusion of cultures that make Fort Kochi a captivating destination for those seeking a blend of history, art, and cultural diversity.
