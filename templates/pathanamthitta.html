TOP DESTINATIONS

Gavi

Gavi is a pristine and off-the-beaten-path destination nestled within the Periyar Tiger Reserve. Known for its dense forests, untouched wilderness, and rich biodiversity, Gavi offers a unique eco-tourism experience. The journey to Gavi itself is an adventure, with visitors traversing through winding roads, spice plantations, and lush landscapes, setting the tone for the untouched natural beauty that awaits.

Gavi is a haven for nature enthusiasts, offering activities such as trekking, bird watching, and wildlife safaris amidst the verdant surroundings. The Periyar River and the lush greenery provide a serene backdrop, and the area is home to diverse wildlife, including elephants, leopards, and various species of birds. Gavi is also known for the serene Gavi Lake, where visitors can enjoy boating while immersing themselves in the tranquility of the pristine surroundings. The sustainable and community-based tourism initiatives in Gavi aim to preserve its natural habitat while providing visitors with an unforgettable experience of Kerala's unspoiled wilderness.

Aramula

Aranmula is a charming village situated in the Pathanamthitta district of India. Steeped in cultural and religious significance, the village is known for its unique traditions and serene ambiance. The annual Aranmula Vallamkali, a traditional boat race held during the Onam festival, is a vibrant spectacle featuring elaborately decorated snake boats, reflecting the deep-rooted cultural heritage of the region.

The Parthasarathy Temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna, stands as a centerpiece in Aranmula. This ancient temple is known for its distinctive Kerala architectural style and is an important pilgrimage site for devotees. Beyond its religious and cultural aspects, Aranmula is also renowned for the craft of making Aranmula Kannadi, a unique handmade metal mirror with cultural significance. Surrounded by the natural beauty of Kerala's backwaters, Aranmula offers a tranquil escape for those seeking a blend of cultural exploration and the serene landscapes of the region.

Konni Forest Reserve

The Konni Forest Reserve, located in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala, India, is a verdant expanse renowned for its rich biodiversity and lush greenery. Spread over an area of approximately 350 square kilometers, this reserve is a part of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The forest is characterized by a mix of tropical evergreen and deciduous trees, creating a habitat for a diverse range of flora and fauna.

One of the notable attractions in the Konni Forest Reserve is the Elephant Rehabilitation Centre, where visitors can witness the gentle giants in their natural habitat. The center is dedicated to the care and rehabilitation of elephants, providing a unique opportunity for eco-tourism and wildlife enthusiasts to observe these magnificent creatures up close. The forest reserve also offers trekking trails, allowing nature enthusiasts to explore its pristine landscapes and discover its diverse plant and animal life. With its emphasis on conservation and eco-tourism, the Konni Forest Reserve stands as a haven for those seeking a harmonious blend of nature, wildlife, and sustainable tourism in the heart of Kerala.

Perunthenaruvi Waterfall

Perunthenaruvi Waterfall is a captivating natural wonder nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Western Ghats. The name "Perunthenaruvi" translates to "The Great Honey Stream," capturing the essence of the waterfall's scenic beauty and the surrounding pristine environment. The waterfall is formed by the Pamba River cascading down a rocky terrain, creating a spectacular sight that attracts both nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Perunthenaruvi Waterfall is renowned for its picturesque setting, with the water cascading over the rocks and forming a pool at the base. The surrounding landscapes are adorned with dense forests, adding to the natural charm of the location. The waterfall is accessible through a scenic drive and a short trek, making it a popular destination for those seeking a day of exploration and relaxation in nature. The area around Perunthenaruvi also offers opportunities for picnics, photography, and enjoying the tranquil ambiance created by the sounds of rushing water and the lush greenery. For those looking to experience the raw beauty of Kerala's Western Ghats, Perunthenaruvi Waterfall stands as a captivating destination.
