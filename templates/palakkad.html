TOP DESTINATIONS

Nelliyampathy Hills

Nelliyampathy Hills, nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India, is a captivating hill station renowned for its pristine beauty and tranquil surroundings. The journey to Nelliyampathy is an enchanting experience, with winding roads offering breathtaking views of lush greenery, tea estates, and orange orchards. As visitors ascend, they are treated to panoramic vistas of the Palakkad Gap, and the region is dotted with viewpoints like Seethargundu and Kesavanpara that provide stunning glimpses of the Western Ghats and the plains below. The cool climate, coupled with the charm of rolling hills and dense forests, makes Nelliyampathy a haven for nature enthusiasts and those seeking a peaceful escape.

Apart from its scenic landscapes, Nelliyampathy is known for its plantations, including tea, coffee, cardamom, and spices, contributing to the region's economic and agricultural significance. The serene environment, coupled with opportunities for trekking and hiking, makes Nelliyampathy Hills an ideal destination for those in search of a rejuvenating retreat surrounded by the natural beauty of the Western Ghats.

Parambikulam Tiger Reserve

The Parambikulam Tiger Reserve, situated in the Western Ghats of Kerala and Tamil Nadu, stands as one of the most well-preserved biodiversity hotspots in India. Spanning across an expansive area, the reserve is characterized by dense forests, diverse flora and fauna, and crystal-clear rivers. Home to the elusive Bengal tiger, along with several other endangered species like the Indian elephant, Indian leopard, and Nilgiri langur, Parambikulam is celebrated for its conservation efforts and commitment to preserving the delicate ecosystem.

Visitors to the Parambikulam Tiger Reserve can immerse themselves in its natural wonders through guided safaris, nature walks, and boat rides along the tranquil Parambikulam Lake. The reserve also boasts an impressive treehouse accommodation, allowing guests to experience the beauty of the wilderness up close. With its focus on eco-tourism and sustainable practices, Parambikulam Tiger Reserve not only serves as a haven for wildlife enthusiasts but also stands as a testament to the importance of preserving and protecting our natural heritage.

Malampuzha Dam

Malampuzha Dam, located in the Palakkad district of Kerala, India, is a multifaceted reservoir that serves as a key water source for irrigation and drinking purposes. Constructed across the Malampuzha River, the dam is a significant engineering marvel, set against the backdrop of the picturesque Western Ghats. Completed in 1955, the dam has not only played a crucial role in water management but has also evolved into a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors with its scenic beauty and recreational facilities.

Surrounding the Malampuzha Dam is a beautifully landscaped garden known as Malampuzha Gardens. The garden is adorned with lush greenery, colorful flowerbeds, and various sculptures and structures. One of the prominent features is the gigantic Yakshi (an enchantress) sculpture crafted by the renowned Indian sculptor Kanai Kunhiraman. Boating facilities on the reservoir, a ropeway offering panoramic views, and an amusement park add to the attractions, making Malampuzha Dam a perfect spot for both nature lovers and families seeking a day of leisure. The dam not only caters to the region's water needs but also stands as a testament to the harmonious integration of nature, engineering, and recreation.

Palakkad Fort

Palakkad Fort, also known as Tipu's Fort, is a historic fortress situated in the heart of Palakkad town in Kerala, India. Constructed in the 18th century by Hyder Ali, the Sultan of Mysore, and later completed by his son Tipu Sultan, the fort holds great significance in the region's history. Strategically positioned on a hillock, Palakkad Fort served as a military stronghold during various conflicts, including the Mysore Wars between the British East India Company and the Kingdom of Mysore. The fort's architecture is a blend of indigenous and European styles, with large stone walls and bastions showcasing its military design.

Within the fort premises, visitors can explore various structures, including a Hanuman temple and a spacious ground. The fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, providing a glimpse into its strategic importance in historical times. Today, Palakkad Fort stands as a cultural and historical landmark, drawing tourists and history enthusiasts who are keen to delve into the rich past of the region. The well-preserved fort is a testament to the architectural prowess of its builders and serves as a reminder of the historical events that unfolded within its walls.
